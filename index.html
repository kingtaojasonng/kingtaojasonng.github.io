About Me

Experience

I am a Senior Data Scientist at ASIC, focusing on machine learning and natural language processing applications. Previously, I was a Senior Analytics Platform Owner at ASIC, managing Databricks, RStudio Servers, Python Servers, and the Data Science Lab. Before that, as a Data Scientist at ASIC, I led the development and implementation of NLP Prospectuses, leveraging NLP to extract insights and enhance document analysis. As a Data Scientist at the University of Sydney's School of Computer Science, I developed a Researcher Collaboration Recommendation System. Before becoming a data scientist, I worked as a Business Analyst at Tabcorp, overseeing the Customer Loyalty Project, and as a Software Engineer at Aristocrat, developing slot machine applications.

Education

I completed a Master of Research at Macquarie University under the supervision of Diego Molla-Aliod, Rolf Schwitter, and Houying Zhu, with a dissertation focused on Bayesian statistics and natural language processing. Prior to this, I earned a Master of Data Science from the University of Sydney, where I collaborated with Roman Marchant, Chao Sun, and Phil Henville on my research project. I also hold a Master of Economics from the University of Sydney, as well as a Master’s in Information Technology and a Bachelor’s in Computer Science from the University of New South Wales.

Research

My research primarily focuses on Bayesian statistics as a framework for understanding causality, with a particular emphasis on unstructured data. I am interested in developing probabilistic models that can infer causal relationships.

Who am I

I was born and raised in Hong Kong, where I spent the first 18 years of my life. After high school, I came to Australia for my studies, initially thinking it would be a short stay. Ideas & Inspirations

Product Management
Have you ever developed an application requested by the business, only to find it is never used after implementation? If so, I would recommend the following books to read:
  • Inspired: How to Create Tech Products Customers Love by Marty Cagan
  • The Lean Startup: How Today's Entrepreneurs Use Continuous Innovation to Create Radically Successful Businesses by Eric Ries
  • Crossing the Chasm, 3rd Edition: Marketing and Selling Disruptive Products to Mainstream Customers by Geoffrey A Moore
  • Escaping the Build Trap: How Effective Product Management Creates Real Value by Melissa Perri
  • The Design of Everyday Things by Don Norman
Bayesian Statistics
I see myself primarily as a computer scientist, with some experience in data science, but not as a statistician. However, learning Bayesian statistics, from my perspective, can be challenging since many resources are geared toward trained statisticians. I have found the following materials to be particularly accessible:
  • This YouTube video explains very clearly between Bayesian and Frequentist.
  • A Student's Guide to Bayesian Statistics by Ben Lambert. The author has created a series of YouTube videos that complement the content of the book.
  • Doing Bayesian Data Analysis: A Tutorial with R, JAGS, and Stan by John Kruschke
  • Statistical Rethinking: A Bayesian Course with Examples in R and Stan by Richard McElreath
Coastal Walks
  • If you are looking to explore Sydney on foot, I highly recommend Sydney's Best Harbour & Coastal Walks by Katrina O'Brien, which features nearly 40 scenic trails.
Bias
  • When I first learned statistics, I encountered the concept of bias, which has a precise mathematical definition. However, textbooks often mention terms like experiment bias or omitted variable bias without fully explaining how they arise. Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman delves into the psychological and cognitive processes behind bias, offering a deeper understanding of why it occurs.
Job Hunters
  • I have transitioned through various careers from software engineer and database administrator to data scientist. What Color Is Your Parachute? by Richard N. Bolles provides valuable insights for anyone looking to make a career change.
Psychology
  • I'm not a psychologist, but I found The Courage to be Disliked: The Japanese phenomenon that shows you how to free yourself, change your life and achieve real happiness by Ichiro Kishimi and Fumitake Koga to be incredibly practical. It highlights how our perspective shapes our happiness and how changing the way we see the world can transform our lives.
Life Skills
  • Communication, influence, and persuasion are essential skills, yet they are rarely taught in schools. How to Win Friends & Influence People by Dale Carnegie offers valuable insights to help you develop them.
  • 7 Habits of Highly Effective People by Stephen R Covey is a must-read for personal growth. If you could pick just one book from the list, this should be it.
